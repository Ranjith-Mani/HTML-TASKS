About


Vinesh Phogat is one of India's most accomplished and celebrated female wrestlers. Born on August 25, 1994, in Balali, Haryana, she comes from the famous Phogat family, known for producing several top-level wrestlers. Vinesh is the cousin of wrestlers Geeta and Babita Phogat, both of whom have represented India at international levels.

Vinesh began her wrestling career at a young age, inspired by her family's legacy and her uncle Mahavir Singh Phogat, who trained her. She quickly rose through the ranks, becoming one of India's leading female wrestlers. Her achievements include multiple gold medals at the Commonwealth Games, Asian Games, and other international tournaments. Notably, she won gold in the 48 kg category at the 2014 Commonwealth Games and the 50 kg category at the 2018 Commonwealth Games.

Vinesh Phogat's career has been marked by resilience and determination. In 2016, she suffered a serious knee injury during the Rio Olympics, which sidelined her for several months. However, she made a remarkable comeback, winning gold at the 2018 Asian Games and qualifying for the 2020 Tokyo Olympics.

In addition to her wrestling accolades, Vinesh is known for breaking barriers and inspiring a new generation of female athletes in India. She has become a symbol of empowerment, advocating for women's rights and equality in sports. Her story has been an inspiration not only in India but globally, showcasing the strength, dedication, and perseverance required to succeed in sports.

Achievements

Vinesh Phogat is one of India's most accomplished female wrestlers, known for her significant contributions to the sport. Here are some of her major achievements:




Commonwealth Games (CWG):

  • Gold Medal (2022) - Birmingham: Vinesh won the gold medal in the Women's 53 kg category.
  • Gold Medal (2018) - Gold Coast: She secured the gold medal in the Women's 50 kg freestyle wrestling.
  • Gold Medal (2014) - Glasgow: She won her first Commonwealth Games gold in the Women's 48 kg category.

Asian Games:

  • Gold Medal (2018) - Jakarta: Vinesh became the first Indian woman wrestler to win a gold medal at the Asian Games, competing in the 50 kg category.
  • Bronze Medal (2014) - Incheon: She won the bronze medal in the Women's 48 kg category.

Asian Wrestling Championships:

  • Gold Medal (2021) - Almaty: She claimed gold in the 53 kg category.
  • Silver Medal (2013) - New Delhi: Vinesh earned a silver medal in the 51 kg category.
  • Bronze Medal (2016) - Bangkok: She secured a bronze medal in the 53 kg category.
  • Bronze Medal (2018) - Bishkek: Another bronze in the 50 kg category.

World Wrestling Championships:

  • Bronze Medal (2019) - Nur-Sultan: Vinesh won her first World Championship medal in the 53 kg category.

Olympics:

  • Tokyo 2020: Vinesh was a strong contender but did not medal, losing in the quarterfinals. She had previously competed in the 2016 Rio Olympics but was forced to withdraw due to a knee injury in the quarterfinals.

National Awards:

  • Arjuna Award (2016): This is one of India's highest sporting honors.
  • Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna (2020): India's highest sporting honor.
  • Padma Shri (2020): The fourth-highest civilian award in India.
